Injera

Description

Injera is a soft, spongy Ethiopian flatbread made from fermented teff flour, giving it a slightly sour taste. Its porous texture soaks up sauces, making it perfect for eating with stews and lentils. Often used as both a base and utensil, injera is essential in Ethiopian meals.

Ingredients

  • Teff flour
  • Water
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Mix teff flour, water, and a pinch of salt, and let the batter ferment for 1-3 days.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat.
  3. Pour a thin layer of batter onto the skillet and cook until bubbles form and the top is set.
  4. Remove from the pan and let it cool before serving with stews or curries.
